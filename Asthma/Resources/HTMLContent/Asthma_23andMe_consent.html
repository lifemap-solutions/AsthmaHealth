WHAT IS A RESEARCH STUDY?

A research study is when scientists try to answer a question about something that we don’t know enough about. Participating in a research study may not help you or others.

People volunteer to be in a research study. The decision about whether or not to take part is totally up to you. You can also agree to take part now and later change your mind. Whatever you decide is okay. It will not affect your ability to get medical care within the Mount Sinai Health System.

Someone is available to explain this research study to you. Feel free to ask any questions before you decide. Any new information that develops during this research study that might make you change your mind about participating will be given to you promptly.

Basic information about this study will appear on the website http://www.ClinicalTrials.gov.  There are a few reasons for this:  the National Institutes of Health (NIH) encourages all researchers to post their research; some medical journals only accept articles if the research was posted on the website; and, for research studies the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) calls "applicable clinical trials” a description of this clinical trial will be available on http://www.ClinicalTrials.gov, as required by U.S. Law. This Web site will not include information that can identify you. At most, the Web site will include a summary of the results. You can search this Web site at any time.



PURPOSE OF THIS RESEARCH STUDY:

The purpose of this study is to advance our understanding of the role of genetics in asthma.

Genes are the basic “instruction book” for the cells that make up our bodies. All genes are made of DNA. The DNA of a person is more than 99% the same as the DNA of any other person; identical twins share 100% of their DNA. By comparing your DNA and health data with that of other people we can identify patterns and relationships in genes and environment to help us better understand health and disease. Thus, the purpose of the AMHA2.0 genetics study is to learn about the connection between DNA and asthma.

This study is designed to allow you to upload and share your genetic data from 23andMe with this research study. You may qualify to take part in this research study because you have consented and are currently participating in the Asthma Mobile Health Application 2.0 (AMHA2.0) study and you are a customer of 23andMe. You have allowed 23andMe to store your genetic data obtained from a saliva or blood sample and are now interested in giving authorization to share your 23andMe genetic data with AMHA2.0.

Participating in this sub-study is totally up to you. It does NOT affect your participation in the Asthma Mobile Health study.

DESCRIPTION OF WHAT’S INVOLVED:

In the past few months, 23andMe has partnered with Apple to build an app feature that allows ResearchKit mobile apps to easily import genetic data from 23andMe. ResearchKit is an open source framework introduced by Apple that allows researchers and developers to create powerful apps for medical research.The AMHA2.0 study team has entered into a collaboration with 23andMe in order to learn more about how DNA and genes impact asthma by incorporating the 23andMe genetics app feature into the AMHA2.0 app. Existing customers of 23andMe who agree to participate in the AMHA2.0 study have the option to share genetic data with AMHA2.0. 

If you agree to participate in this research study, the following information describes what may be involved. 

  1. ELECTRONIC CONSENTING: This consent form will tell you about this study to help you make your choice on whether or not to participate. Please take the time to read this carefully before making your choice. If you have any questions or anything is unclear please contact the study team. If you choose to participate in this study, you will be asked a series of questions to ensure you have enough information about the AMHA2.0 genetics study to make your decision. After submitting answers to the quiz questions you will then be asked to electronically sign this consent form on your phone. You should not give consent or start any part of the study until your questions are answered and you are ready and able to begin.

  2. SHARING GENETIC DATA WITH AMHA 2.0: The AMHA2.0 app will enable you to tap into and share your 23andMe genetic data with the AMHA 2.0 study investigators following the signing of this consent. If you agree to share your 23andMe genetic data with the Asthma Health app your data will be sent from 23andMe computers to your phone and then AMHA2.0 computers using the same security methods banks and hospitals trust to transfer their data. Your 23andMe genetic data will be securely stored and connected to your AMHA2.0 data using a randomly generated code. You will not receive any feedback about your personal genetic information, nor will it or can it replace medical care for your asthma.

  3. AMHA2.0 PARTICIPATION: If you are reading this consent form you have already joined the main AMHA2.0 study, thank you! By completing the surveys about your asthma and health you will help create a rich dataset that scientists can use to identify behaviors that help control or worsen asthma.

  4. 6 MONTH SURVEY: In order to learn more about your views on sharing your genetics data we will ask you to complete a survey 6 months after enrollment in this study.


What we will and will not do with your data

  • De-identification: In order to protect your privacy, we will use a random code instead of your name to identify your AMHA2.0 and 23andMe genetic data. We are required to store your electronically signed consent form with your randomly assigned code, which can link your name to your data. In order to protect your privacy only a very small number of study investigators will have access to your signed consent form and your random code. Furthermore, all of the survey and device information that identifies you will be removed from study data before it is shared with any research team for analysis and will be stored separately from your genetic data and linked through study number.

  • Use in research: Your genetic study data will be transferred to a computer where your genetic and health data can be used by researchers who have obtained permission to use the AMHA Study data. Your study data may be shared with other researchers outside of the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai and the Mount Sinai Hospital ("Mount Sinai"), but we will not share your information with any advertisers or other commercial third parties. We will NOT access your personal contacts, other applications, phone use habits, text message content, personal photos, or websites visited. Data will be stored in a manner that maintains strict information technology procedures to safeguard participant information and to prevent improper access.

    While we will not provide any information regarding your personal genetic data, you can check the project’s web site at asthmamobilehealth@mssm.edu to read about the project’s progress and to see if there are any general results.

REASONABLY FORESEEABLE RISKS AND DISCOMFORTS:

Much like fingerprints, it is possible to identify someone if certain data are put together. While we use very strict data security measures to protect your privacy, there is always a small risk that your data may lead to you being re-identified. As technology advances, there may be new ways of linking data back to you that we cannot foresee today. 

There is a possibility that the genetic data, even when presented without other identifying factors, could allow you to be re-identified, and therefore this research study cannot promise anonymity.

There is a small risk that you may face discrimination on the basis of genetic predispositions that are identified through this project. Sometimes patients have been required to furnish information from genetic testing for health insurance, life insurance, and/or a job. A federal law, the Genetic Information Nondiscrimination Act of 2008 (GINA), generally makes it illegal for health insurance companies, group health plans, and employers with 15 or more employees to discriminate against you based on your genetic information. It is important to note that GINA does not provide protections against discrimination when applying for longer term care insurance, disability insurance, or life insurance.

Since the database contains genetic information, a break in security may also pose a potential risk to blood relatives as well as yourself. For example, it could be used to make it harder for you (or a relative) to get or keep a job or insurance. If your private information was misused it is possible you would also experience other harms, such as stress, anxiety, stigmatization, or embarrassment from revealing information about your family, relationships, ethnic heritage, or health conditions.
